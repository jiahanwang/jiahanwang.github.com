Book List

Books I am reading

JavaScript-the Definitive Guide (6th Edition)

By David Flanagan

There is nothing definitive in the world. However, this book is the closest to the definitive in the world of JavaScript and frontend development.

High Performance Web Sites: Essential Knowledge for Front-End Engineers

By Steve Souders

Although there is a new "faster" edition, the rules in this one are still classic and applicative.

One Hundred Years of Solitude

By Gabriel Garcia Marquez

It is magical; It is realist; It is magical realist! We naturally forget what is real or magic while reading; we just feel and enjoy it.

Some of my favorites

Professional JavaScript for Web Developers (2nd Edition)

By Nicholas C. Zakas

It opened the door to a new world. By reading it, I realize JavaScript is not some easy script language which can only do some animation and variable validation. It is a great programming language and the heart of front-end development.

Designing with the Mind in Mind: Simple Guide to Understanding User Interface Design Rules

By Jeff Johnson

The rules are amazingly easy to understand and apply. Extremely suitable for programmers without design background to improve interface usability quickly.

Snow Country, Thousand Cranes, and The Old Capital

By Yasunari Kawabata

No one in the world could present the special beauty of Japan so precisely except Kawabata. As sensible as himself, his novels convey thoughts quietly and peacefully

Confessions of a Mask

By Yukio Mishima

Mishima was writing himself, the struggle, the sorrow and the solitude. Maybe the main protagonist is dramatic and unbelievable but that is him, just like Mishima himself.

The Hunchback of Notre-Dame

By Victor Hugo

It is not a book. It is history. It is not just a history of France but also a history of human civilization. Actually it is interesting to read about what had happened when the age of printing was coming, as we are facing the digital age.

Books I could never finish

The Story of the Stone, or The Dream of the Red Chamber

By Cao Xueqin

Nobody could truly "finish" this novel. It almost contains everything you want to know about 18th century China, just with enormous mysteries.

LIFE

BY EVERYONE

Life is a book. It's short but hard to read. It sucks but you're gonna love it.
